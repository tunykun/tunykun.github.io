So, thinking about it now. I could do daily blog posts, but they would for the most part probably look the same. So instead of daily, I'll be doing 2 a week. One at the start of the week and one at the end of the week.

Right now I am scheduling 18 hour days for myself. I'm up from 6AM to 12AM. My day is split into 3 parts. I've labeled them JOURNAL WRITING/PLANNING, EXPAND YOUR MIND (EYM), and GET WORK DONE (GWD). EYM is where I do things that aren't necessarily related to mathematics or programming. I do things that will help with like general work-ethic or motivation. This can be whatever. Reading books, watching videos, etc. GWD is where I do everything math and programming related. This is generally split into 2 parts. Studying whatever subject and then working on projects.

With that explained, let's actually talk about my goals for the week.

  • Review 1 chapter of a combinatorics textbook
  • Review 1 chapter of a Data Structures and Algorithm textbook
  • Review 1 chapter of a statistics textbook
  • Study data visualization resources such as matplotlib, seaborn, and plotly
  • Continue working on my C++ project (RPG Maker MV clone)
  • Try to solve at least 3 leet code problems
  • Try to be mindful of distractions, stress and my reaction to it, and sleepiness


I think I've underplanned this week. That being said, I don't understand my own pacing yet. While getting work done is important, I need to make sure I don't act in such a way that will cause burnout. Let's see how this plays out.
